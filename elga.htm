The Expected Lasallian Graduate Attributes (ELGAs) focus on the knowledge, skills, and attributes that graduates should acquire and demonstrate in their course of studies and internships as evidence of accomplishing the school’s vision-mission. These ELGAs also reflect the graduate’s capacity for lifelong learning and transfer of knowledge in the workplace.

ELGA A Lasallian who:
Critical and Creative Thinker
  • Generates ideas with resourcefulness, originality, and insight.
  • Responds to multiple experiences and ideas.
  • Uses innovative problem-solving skills and methods.
  • Constructs and applies knowledge to make meaning and communicate ideas.
Effective Communicator
  • Communicates effectively in a range of contexts.
  • Listens actively and responds appropriately.
  • Communicates in various forms (oral, written, visual).
  • Expresses ideas critically and clearly for a variety of purposes.
Reflective Lifelong Learner
  • Critically reflects on problems/issues to shape ideas.
  • Critically evaluates assumptions and values.
  • Plans, organizes, manages, and evaluates own thinking.
  • Develops awareness of the spiritual nature/religious significance of life.
Service-Driven Citizen
  • Creates products that achieve their purpose and appropriate audience.
  • Develops and incorporates Christian leadership skills.
  • Develops and practices interpersonal skills.
  • Nourishes relationships with God, others, and the environment.
  • Evaluates moral and ethical responsibilities.
